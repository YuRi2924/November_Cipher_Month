TALE 3: THE HANGING BALLOONS

Kazuko had never believed in ghost stories, dismissing them as schoolyard rumors. But when her friend Terumi was found hanging in the park, Kazuko's disbelief began to crack. Terumi had been bright and full of life—suicide didn't make sense. And then, strange rumors started. People whispered they'd seen Terumi's face floating above the park, blank-eyed, a noose dangling beneath her chin.

Kazuko brushed it off until one evening when she saw it herself—a balloon with Terumi's face drifting through the twilight, her smile vacant and eerie. But it wasn't alone. Soon, more faces appeared across town, each balloon an exact replica of someone living, each trailing a noose. And they weren't harmless; they hunted, slowly and patiently, finding and lifting their victims into the sky.

The town unraveled as people hid, only to find the balloons waiting outside their windows, hovering with silent determination. No one could escape. Kazuko knew it was only a matter of time before her own face would appear among them, and each night, she lay awake, haunted by the shadow of her inevitable fate.
